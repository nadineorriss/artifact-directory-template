title:
Multi-Facility Patient Flow Optimization: A Simulation Approach

abstract:
Emergency department (ED) boarding, where admitted patients remain in the ED due to unavailable inpatient beds, contributes to prolonged wait times, degraded care quality, and increased patient mortality. In multi-hospital health systems, understanding patient flow across facilities is essential for identifying bottlenecks and evaluating system-wide capacity. This project develops a multi-facility discrete event simulation (DES) of patient flow across UC San Diego Health's three campuses (La Jolla, Hillcrest, and East Campus) using institutional Admit-Discharge-Transfer (ADT) data comprising 2.1 million events across 985,000 patient encounters over a recent one-year observation period. We define a 12-state model consisting of four care tiers (ED, Floor, ICU, and Boarding) across three campuses and construct a trajectory library of 150,337 real patient paths, where each simulated patient follows an actual historical trajectory with empirical durations. Baseline results identify La Jolla and East Campus as the most chronically constrained facilities, with boarding and ICU resources exceeding 80% utilization for the majority of the simulation period. We then integrate a quantile regression LOS predictor as a transfer trigger and evaluate policies across three thresholds (3, 8, and 12 hours). LOS-triggered transfers reliably eliminate boarding congestion at the source campus, but system-level benefit depends on the choice of source: transfers from La Jolla produce unambiguous improvement, while transfers from smaller campuses introduce load-shifting effects that can amplify bottlenecks at receiving facilities.
